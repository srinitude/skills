# Bounded path check

Owner and backlink: [`../SKILL.md`](../SKILL.md). Read this reference only when the parent
skill detects a weak, stalled, or proxy-driven route.

## Compare routes

1. Restate the outcome and the evidence that would prove it.
2. Identify the current bottleneck and the causal link between the requested work and the
   outcome.
3. Compare only materially different candidates:
   - the requested path;
   - an upstream or root-cause path;
   - reuse of an existing asset, channel, or capability when applicable.
4. Reject candidates that violate a fixed constraint or approval boundary.
5. Rank the remainder by expected contribution, proof strength, reversibility, time, cost, and
   risk.
6. Choose the smallest safe route that can meet the proof threshold.
7. Preserve requested work when it contributes. Ask before a material drop, delay, or
   reprioritization.
8. Set an evidence checkpoint. Re-open the choice when its causal assumption fails or progress
   stalls.

## Signals that a route needs review

- The requested output measures activity rather than the desired result.
- Evidence points to a shared upstream bottleneck.
- The same route has produced no progress across repeated attempts.
- A component passes while the parent outcome remains unproved.
- A familiar procedure is repeated after conditions changed.

## Stop conditions

Stop comparing when another candidate is unlikely to change the decision. Do not list
alternatives merely to display them. Explain a route change when it affects requested work,
commitments, cost, timing, or trust.

If the outcome is weakly inferred and different meanings change the route, ask the single
question most likely to resolve the choice. If uncertainty affects only a reversible detail,
state the assumption and proceed.
